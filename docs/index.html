The U.S. government spends hundreds of billions each year on defense. Where does it go?

By Sam Gauntt

sgauntt@terpmail.umd.edu

June , 2026

What is this story about?

The federal government sends hundreds of billions in defense spending to states across the U.S. each year. Who benefits the most? This story will look into where DOD spending is concentrated, and how population, political ideology and other traits relate to this spread.

Why tell this story NOW?

With President Trump requesting $1.5 trillion for the next federal military budget, an increase of 44%, now is a better time than ever to dig into where the federal government concentrates its military spending. 

Who is your target audience?

This story would ideally be interesting for a wide audience. Given that most states have a certain level of military presence and receive defense spending, seeing where spending is concentrated should be of interest for people across the country. I think people in states receiving less defense spending might actually be more interested, as they’re receiving less of a benefit even though its supported by their tax dollars. 

For this story, I’d talk to: 

  • Rep. Adam Smith (D-WA), ranking member of the House Armed Services Committee and Rep. Mike Rogers (R-AL), chairman of the committee. 

  • Steve Ellis, president of Taxpayers for Commons Sense. He has been sounding the alarm on the impact of such a significant funding increase for the Department of Defense. 

  • Kristy E. Williams, Director of Defense Capabilities and Management for the U.S. Government Accountability Office. She recently oversaw a report into the Pentagon that determined the its leadership did not consider the impact of recent civilian federal worker layoffs on its operations. 

Impact

Trump’s request of $1.5 trillion in funding for the military would be a massive surge over any previous budget. It also coincides with slashes in federal support for many other agencies and departments, rising costs, increasing inflation and layoffs of federal workers. The money for this budget increase would have to come from other sources, such as cuts for other services. And this could impact daily lives of U.S. citizens.

Three interesting takeaways

  1. Fort Worth, Texas, receives by far the most defense spending of any community in the country at about $120 billion from 2020 to 2024. T

  2. Virginia received the most spending per resident, at about $5,800 per Virginia resident.

  3. 2024 was the first year since 2020 that defense spending decreased over the previous year.

Summarize any previous coverage

Most recent coverage has focused on the debate in Congress over Trump’s requested budget. There have been many stories on Democrats and Congressional leadership pushing back against Trump’s request, and what the increase would have for the military overall and its capabilities. Other coverage has compared defense spending in the U.S. compared to other nations, given that we spend more than the next several nations combined. Little coverage I’ve reviewed has focused on the difference between individual states or counties within the U.S.

Photos, video, graphics

  • Photos: Pictures of military installations that will receive a boost in funding or the Pentagon. Also pictures of any lawmakers or sources I interview. 

  • Video: I could perhaps do a vertical video explaining this story. A lot of people on social media are concerned about military spending, so a quick explainer ahead of the budget reconciliation process could be useful.  

  • Graphics: I am going to make a heat map of U.S. counties by total defense spending. I can also make a chart showing spending based on whether states voted for Trump or Harris in 2024, with circles for each state colored depending on how it voted and sized in accordance with its defense funding. This can give viewers a sense if there are any party trends in funding.

Currently, I only have data from 2020-2024, but ideally, I would like to include data from Trump’s second presidency as well.

      state = NAME,
      population = estimate
    )
